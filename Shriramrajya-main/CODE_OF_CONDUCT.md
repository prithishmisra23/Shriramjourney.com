# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Shriram Journey is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for all contributors, users, and community members, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are dedicated to promoting a culture of mutual respect rooted in Dharma principles: **Seva (service), Bhakti (devotion), Gyaan (knowledge), and Samudaya (community)**.

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by the Shriram Journey project, including:
- GitHub repositories and discussions
- Community forums and chat channels
- Project events and meetings
- Social media accounts
- Email communications
- Any other community space

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment:

- **Respectfulness**: Treating all members with dignity and kindness
- **Inclusivity**: Creating an environment where everyone feels welcomed and valued
- **Constructive feedback**: Providing helpful, respectful critique and suggestions
- **Accountability**: Taking responsibility for mistakes and working to improve
- **Active listening**: Genuinely hearing and considering others' perspectives
- **Collaboration**: Working together toward common goals
- **Spirituality with openness**: Honoring Dharma traditions while respecting diverse beliefs
- **Patience**: Understanding that people have different backgrounds and expertise levels
- **Empathy**: Seeking to understand others' experiences and viewpoints

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment**: Any form of unwelcome behavior, comments, or contact based on protected characteristics
- **Discrimination**: Treating people unfairly based on identity, beliefs, or characteristics
- **Hate speech**: Using slurs, derogatory language, or promoting hateful ideologies
- **Sexual harassment**: Unwanted sexual advances, comments, or jokes
- **Threats or violence**: Threatening harm, violent language, or promoting violence
- **Intimidation**: Using power, authority, or numbers to silence or threaten others
- **Trolling**: Deliberately posting inflammatory content to provoke reactions
- **Spam**: Posting repetitive, off-topic, or promotional content
- **Doxxing**: Sharing private information without consent
- **Manipulation**: Deliberately deceiving or manipulating others
- **Disrespect for sacred traditions**: Mocking or deliberately disrespecting Dharma, spirituality, or religions
- **Inappropriate sexual content**: Sharing explicit material in community spaces
- **Abuse of moderation**: Abusing privileges or attempting to circumvent community rules

## Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and taking appropriate corrective action in response to instances of unacceptable behavior.

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Documentation**: Save screenshots or records of the incident
2. **Reporting**: Report to [conduct@shriramjourney.com](mailto:conduct@shriramjourney.com)
3. **Details**: Include:
   - What happened
   - Who was involved
   - When it occurred
   - Links or evidence if available
   - Any impact or concern

4. **Confidentiality**: Reports are handled confidentially. We will not publicly disclose reporter identity without consent.

### Response Process

1. **Acknowledgment**: We acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
2. **Investigation**: We investigate the incident (typically within 1 week)
3. **Decision**: We determine appropriate action
4. **Communication**: We inform involved parties of the outcome
5. **Follow-up**: We check on the situation after action is taken

### Consequences

Community leaders will determine consequences on a case-by-case basis, proportionate to the violation:

1. **Verbal Warning**: For minor first-time incidents
   - Private message explaining the issue
   - Opportunity to correct behavior

2. **Temporary Suspension**: For repeated minor violations or single serious incidents
   - Suspension from project activities (typically 1-30 days)
   - May be longer for serious violations

3. **Permanent Ban**: For severe, repeated, or unrepentant behavior
   - Removal from all project spaces
   - No future participation allowed

4. **Community notification**: Severe violations may be reported to relevant platforms or organizations

### Appeals

If you believe an action taken against you was unfair:

1. Email [conduct@shriramjourney.com](mailto:conduct@shriramjourney.com) with:
   - Your appeal
   - Explanation of why the action was unfair
   - Any new information

2. A different reviewer will assess the appeal within 1 week

## Community Leaders & Responsibilities

Community leaders include:
- Project maintainers
- Moderators
- Event organizers
- Anyone representing the project

**Responsibilities:**
- Model positive behavior
- Act impartially and fairly
- Maintain confidentiality in reporting
- Recuse themselves from decisions involving personal relationships
- Take action against violations
- Document incidents and actions taken
- Stay updated on this Code of Conduct

## Specific Guidelines

### For Code Review

- Focus feedback on code, not the person
- Acknowledge good work and positive contributions
- Provide specific, actionable suggestions
- Ask questions when unclear
- Avoid dismissive language or tone

**Example of good feedback:**
> "This approach could be more efficient. Have you considered using a Set instead of an Array for O(1) lookups? Happy to discuss if you'd like!"

**Avoid:**
> "This code is terrible. You should know better."

### For Discussions

- Stay on topic
- Respect different viewpoints
- Avoid political or religious debates unrelated to the project
- Don't promote non-Dharmic religions or traditions in a superior way
- Distinguish between Dharma principles and personal opinions

### For Translations & Content

- Respect cultural and linguistic differences
- Avoid stereotypes or generalizations about cultures
- Work with native speakers and cultural experts
- Give credit for contributions
- Be open to corrections and improvements

### For Sacred Content

- Treat Dharma traditions, scriptures, and deities with respect
- Acknowledge multiple valid interpretations
- Ensure accuracy in religious/spiritual content
- Consult with experts when needed
- Never mock or belittle spiritual beliefs

- Open for community feedback
